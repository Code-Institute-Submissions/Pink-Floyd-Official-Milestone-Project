Band History

Pink Floyd were formed in London in 1965 and are one of the most commercially successful and influential groups in popular music history. They were founded by students Syd Barrett (guitar and lead vocals) on guitar and lead vocals; Roger Waters (bass and vocals); Nick Mason (drums); and Richard Wright (keyboards and vocals). They rose to fame in the underground music scene during the late 1960’s, and released their debut album: ‘The Piper at the Gates of Dawn’ in 1967. Dave Gilmour (guitar and vocals) joined in December 1967, swiftly followed by Syd Barrett’s departure, due to mental health issues, in April 1968. The remaining four members rose to the pinnacle of the music world with the release of ‘The Dark Side of the Moon ‘(1973), ‘Wish You Were Here ‘(1975), ‘Animals‘(1977) and ‘The Wall’ (1979) and The Final Cut (1983). As a result of creative tensions: Wright left Pink Floyd in 1979, followed by Waters in 1985.

Band Members

Syd Barrett

Born 6 January 1946, died 7 July 2006. He was a founding member of the band and was lead singer, guitarist and songwriter until his departure in April 1968. His departure was reportedly due to mental health issues resulting from experimentation with psychedelic drugs.
Find out more

Roger Waters

Born 6 September 1943. He was a founding member of the band and was a singer, bassist and songwriter. Following Barret’s departure, he became the lyricist and conceptual leader of the band. His growing resentment with stardom was exemplfied by the lyrics and imagery of 'The Wall'.
Find out more

Dave Gilmour

Born 6 March 1946, he joined the band in December 1967 as guitarist, singer and songwriter to replace the increasingly unreliable Syd Barrett. In 2005, he was made a CBE for his services to music and, in 2011, ranked 14th greatest guitarists of all time.
Find out more

Nick Mason

Born 27 January 1944, he was a founding member of the band and their drummer. He is the only member of the band that has featured on every one of the bands albums. He co-wrote some of the bands most popular songs such as ‘Echoes’ and ‘Time’.
Find out more

Rick Wright

Born 28 July 1943, he was a founding member of the band and their keyboard player and vocalist. He and Gilmour took over song-writing duties following Barret’s departure. He left the band in 1981 but returned to the band in 1987. He died 15 September 2008.
